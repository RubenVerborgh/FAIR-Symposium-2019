You can choose where you store
every single piece of data you produce.

You can grant apps and people access
to very specific parts of your data.

Separating app and storage competition
drives permissionless innovation.

The traditional way of building apps
does not work well with decentralization.

Building apps over decentralized data
requires different app techniques.

Solid is an ecosystem of data and apps

Querying data in
decentralized networks

  • How can we query across personal data stores,
    taking into account privacy?
  • How can nodes in a decentralized network
    help each other with caching and querying?
  • How can we conceal latency in applications
    that need data from decentralized sources?
    • Can we approximate and incrementally improve results?

Realizing interoperability
without centralized agreement

  • How to deal with disagreement and inconsistency
    on the public Web?
  • Are shapes the new ontologies?
    • What is the role of semantics?
    • What is the role of shapes?
  • How to reshape data on the fly?

Actor roles in
decentralized networks

  • How to distribute tasks
    between clients and servers?
  • What roles can and should
    intermediaries take up?
    • How does this interact
      with privacy and trust?

Facilitating Linked Data
application development

  • How can we expose Linked Data to developers,
    hiding the complexities of RDF but
    keeping flexibility and unboundedness?
  • How to leverage composable data shapes that
    apps bind to, as opposed to custom data models?
  • How to read and write Linked Data
    according to a specific shape?

Turning personal data
into Linked Data

  • How can we extract personal data
    from third parties?
  • How can we link personal data
    from different third parties together?
